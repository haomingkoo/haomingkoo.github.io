# Portfolio design direction

This homepage should feel like a premium product launch page for an applied AI engineer, not a decorated resume.

## First impression

The first screen has one job: make a recruiter understand the person, the role, and the proof in a few seconds.

- Name: Haoming Koo
- Role: Applied AI engineer in Singapore
- Story: turns messy workflows into shipped AI products
- Proof: 7 years at Micron, AI Singapore, $600M+ aggregate program impact, 8 live apps
- Next step: hire, open live apps, inspect enterprise pattern

## Visual story

Use the cinematic reel as proof of taste and range. The video should not carry important text because generated text can drift. HTML carries the real message.

Storyline:

1. Messy inputs: job posts, market data, maps, tickets, documents.
2. System layer: retrieval, tools, evals, guardrails, traces, human review.
3. Product layer: live apps someone can open and test.
4. Enterprise layer: planning work becomes governed SQL, evidence, root-cause notes, and reviewable handoff.

## Product proof order

Homepage order:

1. Hero with cinematic reel and hiring proof.
2. Three fastest live products: Job Hunter SG, Trader Koo, Japan in Seasons.
3. Public-safe enterprise AI pattern.
4. Stats strip.
5. Architecture story.
6. Full project grid.
7. About, writing, closing contact.

## Motion rules

Motion should explain the work, not decorate the page.

- Use the reel as the big emotional moment.
- Keep scroll animation calm and tied to the architecture story.
- Avoid cursor trails, particles, excessive parallax, or text flying in.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

## Copy rules

Use short, specific sentences.

Avoid:

- inflated launch-deck terms
- vague claims without proof
- private client names or internal system details
- generated-looking rule-of-three filler

Use:

- “live apps”
- “governed text-to-SQL”
- “evidence packs”
- “human review”
- “source checks”
- “evals”
- “public-safe architecture pattern”

## Public sharing boundary

It is acceptable to say “large multinational semiconductor environment” and “semiconductor operations.”

Do not include private screenshots, table names, schemas, process details, customer names, internal hostnames, or metrics that are not already approved.
