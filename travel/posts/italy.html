13 Days Across Italy — A Winter Family Trip

Milan → Florence → Pisa → Rome → Venice. Two weeks, four cities, one leaning tower, zero regrets. Christmas in Milan, NYE in Rome, and a gondola through Venice.

Four of us flew from Singapore, spent Christmas in Milan, saw Michelangelo's David in Florence, rang in New Year's Eve in Rome, and floated through Venice on a gondola. 13 days of art, food, and history with the family.

Winter Italy is a different experience from the summer crowds. The streets are quieter, the Christmas markets are magical, and you can actually stand in front of a painting at the Uffizi without someone's selfie stick in your peripheral vision.

Pro tip: Book trains on Trenitalia or Italo early. We scored Milan → Florence for about EUR 20 per person. That same route can cost EUR 60+ if you book last minute.

Milan

Milan 2 nights · Hilton Milan

We landed on Christmas Eve. Milan in December is cold, sharp, and impossibly stylish. People were wearing coats that probably cost more than our flights. The city was draped in Christmas lights and the Duomo looked like it was designed specifically to be an Instagram backdrop.

Day 1 — Dec 24
Arrival + Christmas Eve
Landed at Malpensa, checked into the Hilton, and walked to the Duomo for the Christmas Eve atmosphere. The piazza was packed with families, street performers, and the smell of roasted chestnuts. We grabbed panettone from a bakery — because when in Italy, you eat panettone, not log cake.
Arrival Christmas
Day 2 — Dec 25
Duomo, Sforza Castle, and The Last Supper
Christmas morning at the Duomo di Milano. We went up to the rooftop terraces — the view of the city with the Alps in the distance is something else. Then walked to Sforza Castle, which houses several museums (including unfinished Michelangelo works). The highlight: Cenacolo Vinciano — Leonardo da Vinci's The Last Supper. You get exactly 15 minutes in the room. Book months in advance or don't bother.
Duomo Da Vinci Culture
Day 3 — Dec 26
QC Termemilano + Train to Florence
Started the morning at QC Termemilano, a thermal spa built inside old Milan tram tunnels. Hot pools, steam rooms, and a rooftop jacuzzi overlooking the city — perfect for a cold December morning. Then we hopped on the Trenitalia to Florence. The train ride is about 1h 45m and the Tuscan countryside views are free.
Spa Train

Florence

Florence 3 nights · 25hours Hotel Florence

Florence is what happens when an entire city decides to be a museum. Every street corner has a building that's older than Singapore by about 500 years. The food is incredible, the leather shops are everywhere, and you will eat your body weight in gelato whether you planned to or not.

Day 4 — Dec 27
Galleria dell'Accademia + Duomo di Firenze
Michelangelo's David is one of those things that looks impressive in photos but absolutely floors you in person. The statue is 5.17m tall and the level of detail is absurd — you can see the veins in his hands. After that, we climbed 463 steps to the top of the Duomo di Firenze. The Brunelleschi dome is an engineering marvel from the 1400s that still makes modern architects jealous.
Michelangelo Architecture
Day 5 — Dec 28
Day Trip to Pisa
Took the regional train to Pisa (about 1h each way, EUR 10 return). Yes, we did the obligatory "holding up the tower" photo. Yes, it was worth it. The Leaning Tower actually leans at 3.97 degrees — they've spent centuries trying to stabilise it. The entire Piazza dei Miracoli (Field of Miracles) is gorgeous. Half-day trip, back in Florence for a Florentine steak dinner.
Day Trip Pisa Food
Day 6 — Dec 29
Uffizi Gallery + Train to Rome
The Galleria degli Uffizi has Botticelli's Birth of Venus, Caravaggio, Raphael, and more Renaissance art than your brain can process in one visit. We spent 3 hours and barely scratched the surface. After lunch, boarded the train to Rome. Next stop: the Eternal City.
Art Uffizi Train

Rome

Rome 3 nights · Mercure Rome Colosseum Centre

Rome is chaos, beauty, and carbs all mixed together. The hotel was right next to the Colosseum — you could see it from the window. Every direction you walk leads to something ancient, crumbling, and absolutely magnificent. Also, Roman pizza is thin and crispy, not the thick Neapolitan style. Both are correct life choices.

Day 7 — Dec 30
Vatican Museum + Sistine Chapel
We did a guided tour of the Vatican Museum and Sistine Chapel. The museum has 54 galleries, so a guide is essential unless you want to wander aimlessly for 6 hours (which is also valid). The Sistine Chapel ceiling is... there are no words. Michelangelo painted it while lying on scaffolding for 4 years. The man was built different.
Vatican Michelangelo
Day 8 — Dec 31
Colosseum + New Year's Eve
Morning at the Colosseum with Attico (top level) access. Standing where gladiators fought 2,000 years ago is a deeply humbling experience. The engineering behind the arena — retractable awnings, underground tunnels, trapdoors — was centuries ahead of its time. NYE dinner at a local trattoria, then watched fireworks over the Roman skyline. Not a bad way to end 2024.
Colosseum NYE
Day 9 — Jan 1
New Year's Day + Train to Venice
Slow morning. Walked around Trastevere and the Roman Forum. Most places were closed for New Year's, which meant the streets were empty and peaceful — a rare sight in Rome. Afternoon train to Venice, our final stop.
Exploring Train

Venice

Venice 2 nights · Hotel Saturnia & International

Venice is not a real place. It's a floating city where streets are water and taxis are boats. You will get lost. That's the point. Every wrong turn leads to a hidden piazza or a canal view that makes you forget you're carrying a 12kg backpack.

Day 10 — Jan 2
Gondola, Doge's Palace, St. Mark's
The gondola ride was EUR 20 (per person split between 4). Thirty minutes of gliding through narrow canals while the gondolier narrates the history of each building. Worth every cent. Then the Doge's Palace — the Secret Itineraries Tour takes you behind the walls into hidden chambers, the prison, and across the Bridge of Sighs. St. Mark's Basilica is gold everywhere. The Pala d'Oro altarpiece alone has over 1,900 gemstones.
Gondola History
Day 11 — Jan 3
Getting Lost (On Purpose)
No plan. Just walked. Found a tiny cicchetti bar where the owner insisted we try everything. Crossed the Rialto Bridge at sunset. Watched the boats go by with a spritz in hand. Sometimes the best travel experiences are the ones you don't plan.
Exploring Food
Day 12–13 — Jan 4–5
Departure
Water taxi to Marco Polo Airport. Flight back to Singapore via transit. Arrived home with sore feet, a full camera roll, and already planning the next trip.
Departure

Practical Tips

Getting Around

  • Trenitalia / Italo: Book 1-2 months ahead for the cheapest fares. High-speed trains connect all major cities in under 2 hours.
  • Open return for day trips: Regional trains (like Florence ↔ Pisa) don't need advance booking. Just buy at the station.
  • Venice vaporetto: Get a 24h or 48h pass. Individual rides are EUR 9.50 each and add up fast.

Booking Attractions

  • The Last Supper: Book 2-3 months in advance on the official site. Slots sell out immediately.
  • Vatican: Book the earliest morning slot. By 10am the queues wrap around St. Peter's Square.
  • Uffizi: Go on a weekday if possible. We went on a Sunday and it was still manageable in winter.
  • Colosseum: The Attico (top level) ticket is worth the extra cost for the view.

Money

  • Exchange rate: At the time of our trip, EUR 1 ≈ SGD 1.42.
  • Card vs. cash: Card accepted almost everywhere. Keep some cash for small shops and markets.
  • Tipping: Not expected in Italy. Service charge (coperto) is usually included in the bill.

Winter Travel Perks

  • Shorter queues at every major attraction.
  • Hotel prices 30-50% lower than summer peak.
  • Christmas markets and festive decorations everywhere.
  • Cooler weather (5-12°C) is actually perfect for walking 20,000+ steps a day.

Italy in winter was a wonderful experience. If you're considering it, I'd highly recommend the winter months — fewer crowds, festive atmosphere, and pleasant walking weather. Hope this itinerary and breakdown helps with your planning!
